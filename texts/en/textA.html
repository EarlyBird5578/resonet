Amplitude – The Power of Impact

1. What is Amplitude?

Amplitude describes the displacement of a wave – how strongly a signal affects the field. The greater the amplitude, the more noticeable, visible, and intense the frequency becomes. It is the measure of intensity.

2. Amplitude Creates Meaning

A weak oscillation often goes unnoticed. Only when a frequency gains enough amplitude can it penetrate fields, reach systems, and leave an impact. Amplitude determines whether a pulse is a whisper or a call.

3. Amplitude Is Not Just Volume

It can manifest in many forms – as emotion, light intensity, pressure, or repetition. It is often felt rather than measured. Some impulses work through presence, not through force.

4. Amplification & Dissonance

Amplitude can combine with other fields – or cancel out. Two identical impulses amplify each other, two opposite ones neutralize each other. This is called interference.

5. Amplitude Is Relationship

Strong amplitude without connection reaches nothing. It needs resonance surfaces. A scream in an empty room fades away – only through feedback does communication emerge.

6. Amplitude Needs Direction

Without frequency, there is no amplitude – only energy without form. And without inertia, it dissipates. Amplitude arises between beginning and persistence. It is the intensity of the in-between.

→ What Follows from Amplitude?

The greater the amplitude, the greater the influence – but also the responsibility. What acts strongly can empower or destroy. That’s why conscious amplitude matters: not just being loud, but being clear.
