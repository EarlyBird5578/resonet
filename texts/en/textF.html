Frequency as the Beginning of Everything

1. What is a Frequency?

A frequency is a repeating motion – a pulse, a rhythm, a vibration. It describes how fast or slow an event repeats, whether it’s a wave, a thought, or a heartbeat. Frequency is movement with rhythm.

2. Origin – The First Impulse

Every frequency starts with an impulse – an event that triggers movement. The moment before something begins to oscillate is a point: silent, yet full of potential. Only with the second beat does rhythm emerge – frequency begins to live.

3. Direction and Speed

Frequency has direction – toward something or away from it. It can expand or contract. And it has speed: the faster, the higher the frequency; the slower, the deeper. We sense this in music, speech, emotions, or light.

4. Forms of Frequency

The simplest forms are:

  • Point – the origin, without oscillation
  • Line – directed impulse, e.g. a laser
  • Wave – cyclic, rhythmic, resonant

These basic forms appear everywhere – in movement, communication, architecture, and even thoughts.

5. Limits of Perception

Our senses only capture a narrow band of the frequency spectrum. What’s too slow seems still – what’s too fast becomes invisible.
A well-known example is the wagon-wheel effect: When something spins faster than our eyes can follow, it may appear still or even rotating backward.
Often, we can only observe the effect in the field – not the object itself. For example, a black hole is definitely there, but it simply oscillates too fast for us to perceive.

6. Frequency as the Foundation of Space & Time

Frequency creates time. Without frequency, there would be no repetition – and thus no sense of time.
Frequency also generates space: Depending on how strongly and in which direction it vibrates, expansion occurs. Where frequency ends, space ends.

7. What Follows from Frequency?

Frequency is the first step. But only through interaction with other frequencies does amplitude arise. And only through resistance does inertia form.
Frequency alone is pure – but not complete. It needs interaction, feedback – resonance.
